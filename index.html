About

I am an undergraduate student majoring in Theoretical and Applied Mechanics at the College of Engineering, Peking University. I am currently advised by Prof. Sheng Mao. I also had the privilege of conducting summer research under the supervision of Prof. Yang Jiao at Arizona State University.

I view mathematics, physics, and computation not as isolated disciplines, but as reflections of nature at different levels, unified by a deep, intrinsic beauty. My academic journey is dedicated to grasping this unity to advance our understanding of the material world.

At the intersection of computational mechanics, applied mathematics, and materials science, I am currently developing frameworks to decode the behavior of heterogeneous materials. My work navigates through hyperuniformity and microstructure modeling, utilizing homogenization theory and stochastic analysis to bridge scales. By solving partial differential equations through rigorous numerical analysis, I aim to translate these theoretical mathematical principles into tangible technological advancements.

I am open to discussions and potential collaborations that explore these intersections.

Contrast expansion formalism and rapidly convergent approximations for transport properties of heterogeneous materials characterized by continuous random fields

Liyu Zhong, Sheng Mao

Physical Review B

Established an exact contrast expansion formalism with Hermite-Wick closure to accurately predict the effective conductivity of heterogeneous media described by continuous random fields.

Construction of digital realizations of anisotropic hyperuniform continuous random fields in two and three dimensions via generalized spectral filtering

Liyu Zhong, Sheng Mao

Physical Review E

Developed a fast, non-iterative spectral-filtering algorithm to generate large-scale anisotropic hyperuniform continuous fields using generalized superellipse masks.

Modeling disordered hyperuniform heterogeneous materials: Microstructure representation, field fluctuations and effective properties

Liyu Zhong, Sheng Mao, Yang Jiao

Acta Materialia

Established a framework for modeling continuously varying hyperuniform materials and revealed how hidden microstructural order suppresses physical field fluctuations and ensures highly deterministic effective properties.
